A Sonnet You'll Never Read

Our love was brief but intensifying,
Like the latest trend, or a shooting star.
We weren’t perfect, but I have yet to find
something as satisfying as what we had.

I can’t take back what I said, or forget your harsh words.
You were right, and I was too,
yet we were both horribly wrong, all at once.

I want to erase you, but I can’t lose
the times your hand warmed mine on winter nights,
art galleries, half-watched movies, and that
time you tried teaching me to ballroom dance,
or when you told me that my blue eyes glow.

It doesn’t hurt anymore, but I still feel that void that you left.
Even though half a year has passed and you’ve moved on.
