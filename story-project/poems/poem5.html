[Untitled]

When I first met him, I was perplexed
Because behind his crooked, wired specs
Is a brilliant mind with an impeccable vision.
His creations for class impressed me every week.
His perspective of people and things
Is so rare and unique,
And I aspired to be like him.
In a world full of mediocrity,
He is the brightest light that I’ve seen in this city.

As time passed, his creativity faded.
The oppressiveness of institution
Left him understandably jaded.
Education systems break people like him
To keep them from soaring too high
Over their peers of flightless birds.
I tried to keep him above the ground,
Save him from circling around the drain.
I lost sleep worrying over his future,
While he found sleep because he gave up on it.

Now the teacher has fallen behind the pupil,
And watching it go down just brings me pain.
His eyes, once gleaming with aspirations
Are now sad and branded by red veins,
Because the joy he once had from creating
Has been replaced by artificial elations.
His brain, once beaming with ideas,
Is constantly dulled by substance after substance.
Week after week, his effort deteriorates,
And his critiques and praise become meaningless.

My hero is his own kryptonite,
And I’ve finally realized now
That there’s nothing I can do to save him.
It truly puzzles me, how
Some gifted humans succeed and grow,
Have their names in headlines and shows,
While others let their talent go stale,
Throw away their dreams, morals, and health,
Allow their life to unravel apart,
And break the people they’ve inspired’s hearts.
